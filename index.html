About Pastor Koshy Samuel

It was a crisp Sunday morning in Limerick, and the Gilgal Pentecostal Church hummed with life.

Families walked in, the scent of fresh rain mingling with the warm, inviting aroma of coffee

in the fellowship hall. At the center of it all stood Pastor Koshy Samuel, his presence gentle yet commanding, like a lighthouse guiding the faithful to safety.

Born and raised in Kerala, India, Pastor Koshy had been no stranger to challenges. His calling came early in life, during a prayer meeting in a small village church where he felt an undeniable pull toward ministry. The journey led him across continents, finally planting him in Limerick, a place that seemed worlds apart from the tropical landscapes of his youth.

Yet, Limerick felt like home.

Under Pastor Koshy’s leadership, Gilgal Pentecostal Church grew not just in numbers but in spirit. Known for his humility and wisdom, Pastor Koshy had a gift for weaving scripture into everyday life. His sermons weren’t just words but lessons that stayed with the congregation long after they left the pews. Whether it was a story from his own life or a parable from the Bible, he had a way of making the message feel personal, as though it was meant just for you.

One chilly winter evening, as the city prepared for Christmas, a struggling family knocked on the church doors. They had come to Limerick seeking a better life but found themselves without food or a place to stay. It was Pastor Koshy who answered their call. He welcomed them in, shared a warm meal, and found them shelter through the church network. His actions spoke louder than any sermon.

“We are the hands and feet of Christ,” he often said. “Our faith is meaningless if it does not move us to love.”

But it wasn’t just grand gestures that defined him. It was the small things: remembering the names of every child in Sunday School, visiting the sick, or simply offering a kind word to a weary soul after service.

Gilgal Pentecostal Church was more than a congregation; it was a family, bound together by faith and love, with Pastor Koshy as its ever-steady shepherd.
